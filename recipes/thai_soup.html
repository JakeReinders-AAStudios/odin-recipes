Thai Soup

Introduction:

This is a Thai Soup I found on an Instagram, her handle is Nines Table. It's incredibly delicious and I've made it many times over the past few weeks. It's also super cheap because if you buy the $7 lamb bones from Coles as opposed to lamb forequarters, then youre getting the same cut of meat and same amount of meat, it's just way uglier.

Ingredients:

Paste:

  • 1 chunk ginger
  • 1 bunch of coriander roots
  • 1 bunch chillies - You can use dried or frozen if that's all you have - or none, if you're scared.
  • 1 small pebble size shrimp paste
  • 1 bunch garlic - again, to taste. All if this is to taste and whatever you have to hand or can afford. It's tough out there.
  • 1 quarter of an onion - any alium is fine. Same drill as above
  • 1 stalk lemongrass - optional if you're rich
  • 1 bit of kaffir lime leaves - terrible grammar. Dont care I can rarely afford it here anyway

Soup:

  • Whatever meat you can get - or none
  • Stock powder or MSG or regular stock
  • Whatever vegetables you can get - or none
  • The rest of the onion
  • More chillies
  • Fish Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Lime juice - I found powdered lime at this little thai grocer but you can use normal lime or lime juice. I bet tamrind would also be awesome

Equipment

I have collected various bits and pieces over the years. Things I love and look after better than my liver

While I don't believe you need anything fancy, I have seen many claypots and mortar and pestles at Op shops and theyre worth investing in, particularly the mortar and pestle, the flavour is so much better pounded than blended or chopped and adds to the visceral feeling of connection you get while cooking.

  • Claypot or regular pot enough to hold all this shit
  • Mortar and pestle - or blender if you dont care about my heartfelt message earlier

Method:

  1. After cleaning ginger and other veg, chuck the peel and shit into the pot of water with the powdered stock or regular stock. Also you can add pork rind in here if you used pork as your protein.
  2. While that's ticking away, start pounding the garlic and coriander roots. Then incrementally add chillies, ginger, lemongrass and any other soup ingredients you're smacking in there.
  3. Clean your meat, fam. Legit it doesnt take long but for stocks like this, put them into ap pot of cold water, bring it to a boil, remove the scum and clean under cold water. You know the drill.
  4. Remove any peels or veg you put in the stock and add add all the paste into the pot, stirring to combine.
  5. Add the meat and chuck a lid on it until whatever meat youre using is as tender as you like, preferably cooked though.
  6. Just before it's done, add your veg and a fuckload of herbs like coriander, mint and or holy basil. Also more chillies and fresh garlic or ginger.
  7. Serve with jasmine rice or try sticky rice ADD LINK.

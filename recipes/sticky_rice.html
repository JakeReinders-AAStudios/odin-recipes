Sticky Rice

Introduction:

I recently learned how to cook sticky rice without needing traditional equipment, which has been a genuine source of joy and comfort throughout the winter soup season. This is a simple recipe and, while slighlty more laborious than regular jasmine rice, is well worth the slightly extra effort.

Ingredients:

Rice:

  • 1 amount of glutinous rice - I never know how much, and the beauty of steaming is that you dont need equal amounts

    • A small note, Coles and most of the supermarkets stock glutinous rice, the brand I use is $5 and is found in the Asian section. If you do have an asian store locally, do yourself and them a favour and buy there. This is a one-ingredient recipe and trust me when I say that quality rice makes the world of difference. The fragrant earthiness of quality sticky rice is paradise in your mouth.

Equipment

I've tried a few online suggestions, but the best that I've found with the stuff that I have is definitely the trusty sieve. If you don't have a sieve, you can also use foil covering the pot tightly and a bunch of holes poked into it.

  • Sieve
  • Pot

Method:

  1. Dump however much rice you want into a bowl.
  2. Wash your rice two or three times to remove dust and other impurities. It'll go from really milky to mostly clear - don't overdo it or you'll risk wasting your time.
    3. >
  3. You need to soak the rice in cold water for like 4-6 hours, or if you're anything like me and have no time, boil some water and add 80% cold and 20% boiling water and soak for as long as you can.
  4. Boil some water in a pot, the amount you use will depend on how deep your pot is. Be careful to not overfill, you don't want the bottom of the sieve touching the water or your rice will go soggy./li>
  5. Cover with foil or a suitable bowl and leave it for ten minutes.
  6. After ten minutes, flip the rice and move it around a bit before covering again.
  7. After another 5 minutes turn off the heat and let it chill for a further ten before serving.
  8. this goes really well with my Thai Soup recipe..
